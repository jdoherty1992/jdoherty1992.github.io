ABOUT
James Doherty is a Musician from Belfast, Northern Ireland. He plays Trombone and Piano.


James started playing Trombone at the age of eight with the City of Belfast School of Music. He played in various Orchestras and Concert Bands including the City of Belfast Youth Orchestra and the City of Belfast Youth Concert Band. James toured the USA on two occasions with the Youth Concert Band and France with the Youth Orchestra. While at the City of Belfast School of Music James achieved Grade 8 ABRSM with distinction and was awarded the Leonard Pugh award. Outside the School of Music he also played with the Ulster Youth Jazz Orchestra.

In May 2013 James graduated from University of Ulster with a BMus. While in Derry he gained significant experience performing with multiple groups as well as a soloist. Most notably at the City of Derry Jazz Festival.

After graduating James moved to Dublin in 2013 to further his musical education at Dublin Institute of Technology where he studied for a Masters in Jazz Performance. During his time there James played with the DIT Symphony Orchestra as well as the Trinity Orchestra on a couple of occasions, most notably on their tour to Berlin in January 2014. James completed his Masters in September 2016 and graduated in February 2017.

In late 2013 James started playing with Booka Brass, an eight piece New Orleans style Brass Band. Starting off playing in small venues they went on to organise and perform sold out shows all over Ireland including Vicar Street, Dublin and four times at the Cork Opera House as part of the Cork Jazz Festival in 2015, 2016 and 2017.

Booka Brass have performed for a number of television shows in Ireland including 'Other Voices', 'The Ray D'arcy Show', and 'Republic of Telly'. In September 2017 they were featured in an 8 part Sky Arts TV series called 'Tony Visconti's Unsigned Heroes'. The show consisted of a collaboration concert in the Union Chapel, London. At this show we got to perform along with the likes of Bob Geldof, Stewart Copeland and Imelda May.

Booka Brass has played in Music festivals like Glastonbury and Electric Picnic and has performed abroad in the UK, France and Germany. They have collaborated with some of Ireland's top acts including Glen Hansard, Lisa Hannigan and James Vincent McMorrow. They have performed twice at the President's Garden Party in the Áras An Uactaráin and have featured in a number of Ireland and Leinster Rugby matches in the Aviva Stadium.

James performs on many occasions with the RTE Concert Orchestra including their live recording session with Niall Horan for his album 'Flicker'. James also participated in the Feis Ceoil as part of the DIT Trombone Quartet and was awarded the Anchor Cup.

In 2018 James joined the Defence Forces as a Military Musician. Being a military musician he provides musical support to the state including state visits to the Áras an Uachtaráin and Military Engagements. James also plays the national anthems at the Aviva Stadium for Ireland’s home games in the Six Nations.
